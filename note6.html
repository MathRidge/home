Prime Factor Trees

Numbers are built from hidden multiplication pieces. A factor tree helps us break a number apart until the smallest pieces are showing.

Break ApartSplit numbers into smaller chunks
Prime PiecesPieces that cannot break smaller
Reduce FractionsCross out matching pieces

1. New Math Names for the Shelves

We can still say top shelf and bottom shelf. Now we also learn their math names.

Top Shelf = Numerator

18 24

The numerator tells how many pieces we have.

Bottom Shelf = Denominator

18 24

The denominator tells how many equal pieces make the whole.

Friendly names: top shelf and bottom shelf.
Math names: numerator and denominator.

2. What Is a Factor Tree?

A factor tree is a picture that shows how a number breaks into multiplication pieces.

  • 18
    • 2
    • 9
      • 3
      • 3
18 → (2) (3) (3)
The tree stops when every ending piece is prime.

3. Safe Tree Method

The safe method is slow, but easy to follow. Try grouping by 2 first. Then try 3, then 5, then 7.

  • 36
    • 2
    • 18
      • 2
      • 9
        • 3
        • 3
36 → (2) (2) (3) (3)
Safe method: keep pulling out 2s first. Then check 3s, then 5s, then 7s.

4. Shortcut Tree Method

Sometimes we can split a number into friendly chunks first, like 4 and 9.

  • 36
    • 4
      • 2
      • 2
    • 9
      • 3
      • 3
36 → (2) (2) (3) (3)
Different trees can grow differently, but the final prime pieces are the same.

5. Break the Numerator and Denominator

To reduce a fraction, break the numerator and denominator into prime pieces.

18 24

Numerator Tree: 18

  • 18
    • 2
    • 9
      • 3
      • 3
Top: (2) (3) (3)

Denominator Tree: 24

  • 24
    • 2
    • 12
      • 2
      • 6
        • 2
        • 3
Bottom: (2) (2) (2) (3)

6. Cross Out Matching Pieces

If the same multiplication piece appears on the top and bottom, cross out the match.

Top: (2) (3) (3)
Bottom: (2) (2) (2) (3)
Remaining: (3) (2)(2) → 3 4
18/24 reduces to 3/4. We did not change the amount. We changed the fraction name.

7. Practice

Break the number into prime pieces. Then use the matching pieces to reduce.

12/20
Answer 12 → (2)(2)(3)
20 → (2)(2)(5)
Cross out (2) and (2).
Remaining: 3/5
15/25
Answer 15 → (3)(5)
25 → (5)(5)
Cross out (5).
Remaining: 3/5
14/21
Answer 14 → (2)(7)
21 → (3)(7)
Cross out (7).
Remaining: 2/3
36/60
Answer 36 → (2)(2)(3)(3)
60 → (2)(2)(3)(5)
Cross out (2), (2), and (3).
Remaining: 3/5

The Sign Outside the Box

A special note about signs, size, and clear thinking.

1. What is a Term?

A term is a sign and a number together.

+4
−3
+8
Do not separate the sign from the number. The sign tells the direction. The number tells the size.

2. Hidden Plus

If the first number has no sign, it is positive.

4
→
+4
7
→
+7

Remember

The leading plus sign may be hidden, but it is still there.

3. Same Sign Means Add Inside the Box

If both signs are the same, add the sizes inside the box.

Positive + Positive

4 + 5
+ 4 + 5 =
+9

Negative + Negative

−2 − 3
− 2 + 3 =
−5
Same sign means they are on the same team. They add their sizes together. Their shared sign stays outside the box.

4. Different Signs Means Bigger Size Leads

If signs are different, compare the sizes.

Example A

3 − 8

3 means +3.

8 is bigger than 3.

The 8 has a negative sign.

− 8 − 3
= −5

Example B

−4 + 6

6 is bigger than 4.

The 6 has a positive sign.

+ 6 − 4
= +2
The bigger size leads. Its sign stays outside the box. Inside the box, subtract big minus small.

5. The Sign Outside Becomes the Answer Sign

− 8 − 3 =
−5
+ 6 − 4 =
+2

Big Rule

The sign outside the box becomes the sign of the result.

6. Practice Pattern

Ask these questions every time:

1. Same sign or different sign?

2. Who has the bigger size?

3. What sign stays outside?

4. Do we add or subtract inside the box?

Math is pattern. Pattern builds logic. Logic builds confidence.

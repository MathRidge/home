When the same value repeats many times, do not count every copy one by one. Name the repeated value, split the number of copies into a friendly chunk, and add the chunk totals.

1. Name itSee copies × value.
2. Split copiesUse 5 plus the leftovers.
3. Add chunksSmall totals make the big total.

01 First, Name the Repetition

A long repeated-addition line can tire the eyes. Compress it into one calm name: copies × value.

→
7(2)
Focus cue: the outside number tells how many copies. The number inside the parentheses tells what value repeats.

04 Why the 5-Chunk Helps

Five copies are quick to build. Then the leftover copies are small enough to handle without losing the pattern.

7→(5 + 2)

five copies plus two leftover copies

8→(5 + 3)

five copies plus three leftover copies

9→(5 + 4)

five copies plus four leftover copies

This is not about making the arithmetic harder. It is about making the structure easier to see.

06 Open and Close the Group

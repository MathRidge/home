Fraction Multiplication and Division Through Factor Trees

Before we multiply big numbers, we break them into prime pieces. Then we cross out matching pieces from the top shelf and bottom shelf.

Factor FirstBreak numbers into 2, 3, 5, and 7 pieces
Reduce EarlyCross out matching pieces before multiplying
Flip to DivideDivision turns into the same multiplication process

7.a Multiply Fractions: Break Apart Before Multiplying

When multiplying fractions, the top shelves multiply together and the bottom shelves multiply together. But we do not need to multiply right away.

25 × 256 → 2 × 255 × 6
Main idea: do not rush into 50/30. Break the numbers apart first.

Guided Example for 7.a

25 × 256

Step 1: Put the top shelves together and bottom shelves together.

2 × 255 × 6

Step 2: Break into prime pieces.

Top: (2) (5) (5)
Bottom: (5) (2) (3)

Step 3: Cross out matching pieces.

Top: (2) (5) (5)
Bottom: (5) (2) (3)
Remaining: 53
Answer: 2/5 × 25/6 reduces to 5/3. We used the same prime factor tree thinking from Note 6.

7.b Divide Fractions: Flip the Second Shelf

Dividing by a fraction changes the second fraction. Flip the second fraction's shelves, then continue exactly like 7.a.

25 ÷ 625 → 25 × 256
Only the fraction after the division sign flips. After that, the problem becomes multiplication through factor trees.

Guided Example for 7.b

25 ÷ 625

Step 1: Flip the second shelf.

25 × 256

Step 2: Continue like 7.a.

Top: (2) (5) (5)
Bottom: (5) (2) (3)

Step 3: Remove matching pieces.

Remaining: 53
Answer: 2/5 ÷ 6/25 becomes 2/5 × 25/6, then reduces to 5/3.

Final Memory

7.a Multiplication

Put top shelves together. Put bottom shelves together. Break into prime pieces. Cross out matching pieces.

7.b Division

Flip the fraction after the division sign. Then continue using the same 7.a multiplication method.

One big idea: factor first, reduce early, then multiply only what is left.

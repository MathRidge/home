Fraction Loom Manual

Use one calm path: turn division into multiplication, stack the shelves, break the numbers into prime pieces, cross matching pieces, then multiply only what remains.

01 Start with One Calm Question

Before solving, ask: is this multiplication or division? Multiplication is ready to stack. Division must be changed first.

If you see ×Stack the top shelves and bottom shelves.
If you see ÷Flip only the second fraction, then change to ×.
Focus checkpoint: do not multiply yet. First choose the correct setup.

02 Multiplication: Stack the Shelves

When fractions multiply, the top shelves join together and the bottom shelves join together.

25 × 256 → 2 × 255 × 6
The setup is correct, but it is not finished. The next move is to factor, not to rush into 50/30.

03 Factor First, Then Cross Matches

Break each shelf into prime pieces. Matching top and bottom pieces can cross out before you multiply.

Top shelf
255
Bottom shelf
523
Leftover pieces → 53
Say it: factor first, cross matches, multiply leftovers.

04 Division: Flip Only the Second Fraction

Dividing by a fraction means multiply by its flipped version. The first fraction stays the same.

Start
25÷625
→
Flip the second fraction
25×256
Only the fraction after the division sign flips. Then the problem follows the multiplication path.

05 Division Uses the Same Finish

After the flip, continue exactly the same way: stack, factor, cross, finish.

1Flip

25 ÷ 625 becomes 25 × 256.

2Factor

Top: 2 × 5 × 5
Bottom: 5 × 2 × 3

3Cross

Cross a 2 with a 2. Cross a 5 with a 5.

4Finish

The leftover fraction is 53.

06 Watch the Common Trap

The most common mistake is flipping too much. Keep the first fraction still. Flip only the fraction after the division sign.

Do not flip both52 × 256
Do not keep ÷25 ÷ 256
Correct setup25 × 256
Memory line: first stays, second flips, division becomes multiplication.

07 The Fraction Tree Rhythm

Use this short path whenever your attention starts to drift.

1Notice

Is it × or ÷?

2Flip

Only if it is division.

3Stack

Top with top. Bottom with bottom.

4Factor

Break into prime pieces.

5Cross

Remove matching pieces.

6Finish

Multiply what remains.

Short version: notice → flip if needed → stack → factor → cross → finish.

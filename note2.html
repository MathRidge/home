Sign Compass Manual

Long expressions feel easier when every number joins a team. Your job is not to chase every operation. Your job is to keep signs attached, collect teams, then compare totals.

1. Mark signsEach number keeps its own sign.
2. Build teamsAdd positive sizes and negative sizes separately.
3. Compare totalsThe larger team gives the final sign.

01 Give Every Number a Team

Start by slowing the expression down. Read each signed number as one complete term.

+3 −2 +5 −8 +1
Focus cue: the sign belongs to the number after it. Say “positive three,” “negative two,” “positive five.”

02 Sort the Two Teams

Do not solve left to right yet. First, place the signed numbers into two calm groups.

☀️ Positive Team
+3+5+1
3 + 5 + 1 = 9
🌙 Negative Team
−2−8
2 + 8 = 10
The team signs stay on the outside. Inside each team, add the sizes only.

03 Let the Bigger Team Lead

Now compare the team sizes. The larger team gives the final outside sign.

Positive size: 9
vs
Negative size: 10

Negative team is larger by 1

−10 − 9→−1
Attention anchor: choose the outside sign before doing the subtraction. This protects the final answer.

04 If the Teams Match, the Answer is Zero

Sometimes the positive and negative teams have the same size. When both teams are equal, they cancel out.

+9
=
−9
0
Zero means neither team wins. There is no positive or negative outside sign needed.

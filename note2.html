Positive Negative Showdown Note

Long expressions may look confusing at first. But math becomes easier when every number keeps its sign. We are not solving one operation at a time. We are collecting signed terms.

Big Idea: Every Number Carries Its Sign

In a long expression, the sign belongs to the number after it.

+3 - 2 + 5 - 8 + 1

So instead of seeing one long operation, we see a list of signed terms:

+3, -2, +5, -8, +1

Since addition is commutative, the signed terms can move around. We can group all the positive terms together and all the negative terms together.

Worked Example

+3 - 2 + 5 - 8 + 1
Positive Team
+3 + 5 + 1 = +9
Negative Team
-2 - 8 = -10

Now the problem becomes:

+9 + (-10)

The signs are different, so use Build the Box.

10 - 9 = 1

The bigger size is negative.
So the final answer is -1.

Tip: Write the negative sign first before subtracting.
This helps you remember the final answer is negative.

Common mistake:
Forgetting the negative sign at the end.

Math Ridge Rules

  1. The sign belongs to the number after it.
  2. Turn subtraction into adding a negative term.
  3. Group the positive terms together.
  4. Group the negative terms together.
  5. Add each team first.
  6. Use Build the Box to combine the two teams.

Quick Version

  1. Collect the positive team.
  2. Collect the negative team.
  3. Add each team.
  4. Use Build the Box to combine the teams.

Practice Problems

Type your answer with the correct sign.
Example: +5, -3, or 0

Negative Sign Pairs

Stacked signs are easier when you pause before doing the number work. Look at one term, count its negative signs, fix the sign, then solve the problem.

1. Look closeStay inside one term first.
2. Count negativesEven makes positive. Odd makes negative.
3. Then solveAfter the sign is clean, finish the numbers.

01 Focus on One Term First

Do not let a long problem pull your eyes everywhere. First, find the small sign stack attached to one number.

One term to inspect
−(−2)
Only this part needs a sign fix.
Attention cue: cover the rest of the problem in your mind. Ask, “How many negative signs are in this one stack?”

02 Hidden Positive and Hidden 1

A positive sign can be hidden. A factor of 1 can also be hidden because multiplying by 1 keeps the same number.

+5can be written as5
1 × (+5)becomes+5
−(−5)means(−1)(−5)
This is why a minus sign in front of parentheses can act like a hidden −1.

06 When the Next Term Is Negative

A plus sign can add a negative term. That means the negative team keeps growing.

Attention cue: if the signs are fixed but the problem still has two teams, go back to Build the Box.

Negative Sign Pairs

This note helps you manage signs. We will keep the numbers small, so your brain can focus on the signs.

Big Idea: Positive Signs Can Be Hidden, a product of 1 can also be hidden!

Multiplying by 1 does not change the number.

1 × (+5) = +5

Positive signs are usually hidden in math.

+5 = 5

So the number 5 secretly means:

1 × (+5)

The hidden 1 means one group of the number. The hidden + means the number is positive.

Therefore, a negative sign in front of a term can also mean multiplication by negative one.

-1 × (-5) = -(-5)

So the expression:

-(-5)

can be seen as:

(-1) × (-5)

Two Negative Signs Make a Positive

When two negative signs are inside the same term, they can pair together. A pair of negative signs makes a positive sign.

−
−
→
+
−(−2) = +2 = 2

After the plus sign is formed, the plus sign becomes hidden. That is why the answer is written as 2, not always +2.

Odd or Even Negative Signs

Count only the negative signs that belong to the same term.

Even number of negative signs → positive
Odd number of negative signs → negative

This is only about the sign first. After the sign is clear, then we solve the number.

Worked Example 1: Double Negative

3 − (−2)

The part −(−2) has two negative signs.

−(−2) = +2 = 2

Now the problem becomes:

3 + 2 = 5

Final answer:

5

Worked Example 2: Negative Plus Negative

−4 + (−2)

The plus sign tells us to add the next term. The next term is negative.

−4 + (−2) = −4 − 2

Both terms are negative, so they are on the same team. Add the sizes and keep the negative sign.

−(4 + 2) = −6

Worked Example 3: Three Negative Signs

−(−(−2))

Count the negative signs in the same term.

3 negative signs → odd → negative

Two negative signs pair up and become a hidden positive. One negative sign is left.

−(−(−2)) = +(−2) = -2

Math Ridge Rules

  1. A positive sign can be hidden.
  2. A hidden positive number is still positive.
  3. Two negative signs pair together to make positive.
  4. After a positive sign is made, it can become hidden.
  5. Odd negative signs make the term negative.
  6. Even negative signs make the term positive.
  7. After the sign is clear, use Build the Box if you need to combine terms.

Quick Version

  1. Count the negative signs.
  2. Even means positive.
  3. Odd means negative.
  4. Then finish the problem.

Practice Problems

Type your answer. Positive answers can be written with or without the plus sign. Example: 5 and +5 are both correct.
